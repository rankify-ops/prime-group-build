Our Services

Types of NDIS Home Modifications

We deliver a full range of home modifications to support participants with their accessibility and independence needs.

Accessible Bathrooms

Walk-in showers, grab rails, accessible toilets, non-slip flooring, and adjustable-height fixtures designed for safe, independent use.

Ramps & Access

Ramp installations, step modifications, and pathway upgrades for wheelchair and mobility aid access to your home.

Doorway Widening

Widen doorways and hallways to accommodate wheelchairs, walkers, and other mobility aids throughout your home.

Kitchen Modifications

Lowered benchtops, accessible cabinetry, adjustable shelving, and workspace modifications for wheelchair users.

Handrails & Grab Rails

Strategically placed handrails and grab rails throughout the home for stability and fall prevention in bathrooms, hallways, and living areas.

Structural Modifications

Larger-scale modifications including room reconfigurations, ceiling hoists, and purpose-built accessible additions to meet complex needs.

Our Process

How the NDIS Modification Process Works

We make the process straightforward, working closely with you, your support coordinator, and your occupational therapist.

1. Initial Consultation

We meet with you and your support coordinator or occupational therapist to understand your needs and assess your home.

2. Quote & NDIS Approval

We prepare a detailed quote aligned with the OT's recommendations and submit it for NDIS funding approval.

3. Construction

Once approved, our team completes the modifications efficiently and respectfully, keeping the participant informed throughout.

4. Handover & Support

We walk through the completed modifications with you and your OT, ensuring everything meets the approved specifications and your needs.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about NDIS home modifications.

Are you a registered NDIS supplier?

Yes, Prime Group Building is a registered NDIS supplier and registered builder in Victoria. We can work directly with your plan manager or the NDIA for payment, and we understand the full NDIS process from quoting to completion.

What types of home modifications does the NDIS fund?

The NDIS can fund a range of home modifications including accessible bathrooms, ramps, handrails, doorway widening, kitchen modifications, ceiling hoists, and structural changes. Modifications must be recommended by an occupational therapist and approved as part of your NDIS plan.

Do I need an occupational therapist's report?

Yes, most NDIS-funded home modifications require an assessment and report from a registered occupational therapist. The OT assesses your needs and recommends specific modifications. We work closely with OTs to ensure our quotes align with their recommendations.

How long do NDIS home modifications take?

The construction timeline depends on the scope of work. Minor modifications like grab rails can be completed in a day, while bathroom renovations or structural changes may take 2 to 6 weeks. The NDIS approval process can add additional time before construction begins — we help streamline this as much as possible.

Can you work with my support coordinator?

Absolutely. We regularly work with support coordinators, plan managers, and occupational therapists. We're happy to liaise directly with your support team to ensure the process is smooth and the modifications are exactly what's needed.

Do you handle the NDIS paperwork and approvals?

We prepare detailed quotes formatted for NDIS submission and work with your support coordinator to ensure all documentation meets NDIS requirements. While the approval decision sits with the NDIA or your plan manager, we make the process as straightforward as possible from our end.

Need NDIS home modifications?

Contact us today to discuss your needs. We work with participants, support coordinators, and occupational therapists to deliver quality accessible modifications.
